# CyberQuest Level Assessment Questions

This document contains a comprehensive set of 3 assessment questions (2 multiple-choice and 1 scenario-based) for each of the 5 levels in CyberQuest. These questions test understanding of key cybersecurity concepts taught in each level.

---

## Level 1: The Misinformation Maze
**Learning Focus**: Debunk fake news and stop misinformation from influencing an election

### Multiple Choice Questions (2)

**Question 1:** What is the PRIMARY red flag that indicates a news article might be fake?
- A) It's published on social media
- B) Missing author credentials and suspicious domains
- C) It contains images
- D) It's written in formal language

**Answer:** B

---

**Question 2:** What should you do when you encounter a news article with an extremely sensational headline?
- A) Share it immediately because it's important
- B) Verify the source credibility and check multiple outlets
- C) Ignore it completely
- D) Only read articles with boring headlines

**Answer:** B

---

### Scenario-Based Question (1)

**Scenario:**
You receive an article with the headline "BREAKING: Election Commissioner Caught Accepting Bribes - EXCLUSIVE FOOTAGE!" The domain is electionwatchdogph.org, there's no author name, and the article uses phrases like "They don't want you to know this!" What should you do?

- A) Share it immediately to warn others
- B) Verify with established sources first
- C) Assume it's fake and ignore it
- D) Click to watch the footage

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Multiple red flags present: suspicious domain, missing author credentials, and emotional manipulation tactics. Always cross-reference with established news sources before believing or sharing.

## Level 2: Shadow in the Inbox
**Learning Focus**: Spot phishing attempts and practice safe email protocols

### Multiple Choice Questions (2)

**Question 1:** What is a major red flag in a phishing email?
- A) Professional formatting
- B) Personalized greeting with your name
- C) Generic greeting like "Dear Customer"
- D) Company logo

**Answer:** C

---

**Question 2:** What is a common phishing tactic?
- A) Creating false urgency
- B) Providing detailed contact information
- C) Using proper grammar and spelling
- D) Including legitimate customer service numbers

**Answer:** A

---

### Scenario-Based Question (1)

**Scenario:**
You receive an email: "URGENT: Your PayPal Account Has Been Limited." Sender: security@paypal-verification.com. The link shows http://paypal-secure-verify.tk/login when you hover. What should you do?

- A) Click the link immediately
- B) Reply asking if it's legitimate
- C) Contact PayPal directly through their website
- D) Forward it to your contacts

**Answer:** C

**Explanation:** This is a phishing attempt with red flags: mismatched domain (paypal-verification.com vs. paypal.com), false urgency (24-hour deadline), and suspicious .tk link. Never click suspicious links. Always contact companies directly through their official website.

## Level 3: Malware Mayhem
**Learning Focus**: Isolate infections and perform digital cleanup during a gaming tournament

### Multiple Choice Questions (2)

**Question 1:** What is the most important consideration when responding to a malware outbreak?
- A) Speed is the only priority
- B) Balance speed with accuracy to avoid false positives
- C) Quarantine everything suspicious immediately
- D) Wait until you're 100% certain about each file

**Answer:** B

---

**Question 2:** What is the primary risk of quarantining legitimate files during incident response?
- A) Slows down the investigation process
- B) Damages system functionality and reputation
- C) Makes malware harder to find
- D) Increases file storage requirements

**Answer:** B

---

### Scenario-Based Question (1)

**Scenario:**
During a malware outbreak, you identify several suspicious processes running. You've already terminated two malicious processes correctly, but you accidentally terminated a legitimate antivirus process. What should be your next action?

- A) Continue terminating all suspicious processes as quickly as possible
- B) Slow down and carefully verify each process before taking action
- C) Restart the system immediately
- D) Ignore the mistake and focus on the remaining threats

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** The false positive indicates you need to be more careful. While speed is important in incident response, accuracy is crucial to avoid damaging system functionality and losing credibility. Take time to verify process indicators, check against known legitimate software, and balance urgency with precision to prevent further false positives.

## Level 4: The White Hat Test
**Learning Focus**: Practice ethical hacking and responsible vulnerability disclosure

### Multiple Choice Questions (2)

**Question 1:** What is responsible disclosure?
- A) Immediately publicizing all vulnerabilities found
- B) Selling vulnerability information to competitors
- C) Reporting vulnerabilities to the vendor following ethical protocols
- D) Keeping vulnerabilities secret indefinitely

**Answer:** C

---

**Question 2:** Which Linux command reads file contents?
- A) open
- B) cat
- C) read
- D) view

**Answer:** B

---

### Scenario-Based Question (1)

**Scenario:**
You've discovered a critical SQL injection vulnerability in a voting system. An anonymous group offers payment to disclose it immediately before a patch. A journalist also wants the story. What do you do?

- A) Accept payment and disclose immediately
- B) Follow responsible disclosure to vendor first
- C) Keep the vulnerability secret
- D) Sell to the highest bidder

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Refuse the bribe (maintains ethical integrity). Follow responsible disclosure: notify vendor first, give reasonable timeline for patch development, coordinate with election officials if critical. Premature disclosure creates public safety concerns. Report bribe attempt to authorities.

## Level 5: The Hunt for The Null
**Learning Focus**: Use advanced digital forensics to expose The Null's identity

### Multiple Choice Questions (2)

**Question 1:** What is chain of custody in digital forensics?
- A) The physical location of evidence
- B) Documentation tracking who handled evidence
- C) The encryption method used
- D) The file backup procedure

**Answer:** B

---

**Question 2:** Why is hash verification important in digital forensics?
- A) To encrypt evidence
- B) To verify evidence integrity
- C) To speed up analysis
- D) To compress files

**Answer:** B

---

### Scenario-Based Question (1)

**Scenario:**
You need to analyze a suspect's laptop hard drive. What is the correct first step in the forensic process?

- A) Start examining files on the original drive
- B) Create a forensic image and verify its integrity
- C) Search only for obvious evidence
- D) Begin writing your report immediately

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Always create a forensic image using a write-blocker first, then verify the image's integrity with hash values. Work only on copies of the image, never on the original evidence. This preserves evidence integrity and maintains chain of custody for legal proceedings.

## Assessment Scoring Rubric

### Multiple Choice Questions
- Each correct answer: 1 point
- Total possible per level: 2 points

### Scenario-Based Question
- Correct answer: 5 points
- Total possible per level: 5 points

### Overall Level Assessment
- **Per Level**: 7 points (2 MC + 5 scenario)
- **Total across all levels**: 35 points
- **Passing score per level**: 5 points (70%)
- **Excellent per level**: 7 points (100%)

## Usage Guidelines

### For Instructors
- Use these questions for formative assessment during gameplay
- Adapt scenario complexity based on student level
- Encourage detailed explanations in scenario responses
- Use scoring rubric consistently
- Provide feedback on technical accuracy and reasoning

### For Students
- Answer both multiple choice questions after completing each level
- Read the scenario carefully and consider all options
- Select the most accurate answer
- Review explanations to understand the concepts
- Apply the knowledge learned from gameplay

### Integration with CyberQuest
- Questions presented as post-level assessments
- 3 questions per level for quick evaluation
- Tie assessment performance to in-game progression
- Use as knowledge check before advancing to next level
